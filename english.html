WHO?

Perennial student. Creative problem solver. Career changer. Geek choir alto. Dog person without a dog.

It was the summer 2018, and I decided to pursue a career change. I wanted to find a job where I could constantly learn and evolve professionally. Creative problem solving, logical thinking and working with machines and computers has always been quite crucial part of both my job and personality, so it wasn’t hard to figure out where my path should take me. For the first six months I studied independently, and roughly two years ago I began my studies at Haaga-Helia University of Applied Sciences. If everything goes according to plan, I will finish my bachelor’s degree in Business Information Technology next year.

To my delight I’ve discovered that I’m still capable of learning and combining things fast. It gives me a huge amount of energy when I’m able to solve a problem that I’ve tried to figure out long, or when I learn new ensemble of things that I haven’t even known to exist before. I want to throw myself into this new career properly, work in real-life projects, train and evolve in cases that are relevant and useful to learn and master in this field. I want to understand more.

When I began my career change project, I figured I would simply learn to code. But this turned out to be so much more. Sure, coding is great fun though I’m still very new in it, but user interface design and user experience studies have really piqued my interest. I have always loved to figure out new ways to do things better, faster and simpler and now I feel I have a permission - nay, obligation - to do so. How cool is that?! My future dream job would be a mix of UI/UX-design and front-end, work that aims to offer highly functional solutions, that help clients’ life

So, what I, such a beginner with lots of enthusiasm but alas, so little experience, could offer? Well, I can offer paper conservator’s endless interest to details and tasks that require perseverance; former journalist student’s curiosity, respect to deadlines and literal skills; main editor’s project management- and teamwork abilities, and layout executioner’s love for adjustments and finetuning. My bright-eyed belief that there is nothing I can’t do if just given...

...a chance to learn and ask.
